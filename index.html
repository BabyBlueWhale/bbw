How the Raydium Pool Was Created

Here’s how someone could have bought your token $BBW and created a concentrated liquidity pool on Raydium using SOL as the paired token:

Step 1: Buying Your Token ($BBW)

Purchase on Pump.fun: The user purchased $BBW tokens from your official platform, Pump.fun. This could be done by exchanging a cryptocurrency like SOL for $BBW. After completing the transaction, the $BBW tokens were transferred to the user's Solana wallet.

Step 2: Creating a Concentrated Liquidity Pool on Raydium

Once the user had $BBW tokens in their wallet, they followed these steps to create a concentrated liquidity pool on Raydium:

Accessing Raydium: The user visited the Raydium platform and connected their Solana wallet, where the $BBW and SOL tokens were stored.

Navigating to the Liquidity Pool Section: The user went to the "Liquidity" section on Raydium and chose the option to create a concentrated liquidity pool.

Selecting $BBW and SOL for the Pool: The user selected $BBW as the first token and paired it with SOL, a commonly used cryptocurrency on the Solana blockchain. This pair ($BBW/SOL) allows traders to swap between $BBW and SOL.

Setting the Price Range: In a concentrated liquidity pool, the user can specify a range within which their liquidity will be active. For example, they might set their liquidity to be active only when $BBW is trading within a certain price range relative to SOL. By concentrating liquidity in a specific range, the user aims to maximize the potential trading fees when $BBW trades within that range.

Depositing $BBW and SOL: The user then deposited their $BBW tokens along with an equivalent value of SOL into the pool. The amount of each token would depend on the current market price at the time of deposit. In return, the user received Liquidity Provider (LP) tokens representing their share of the concentrated liquidity pool.

Uncertainty About Earnings: It’s important to note that you do not have visibility into whether the user is earning fees from this pool. Whether or not they earn depends on several factors:

  • Trading Volume: If there’s significant trading activity within the price range they set, they could earn fees. However, if there’s little or no trading within that range, their earnings might be minimal or nonexistent.
  • Market Movements: If the price of $BBW moves outside the range they selected, their liquidity may no longer be active, meaning they wouldn’t earn fees until the price returns to their specified range.

Impact on Your Token: The creation of this concentrated liquidity pool on Raydium means that:

  • Increased Accessibility: $BBW is now available for trading on Raydium, increasing its accessibility to a broader audience.
  • Potential Arbitrage: With $BBW available on both Pump.fun and Raydium, price differences between the platforms might lead to arbitrage opportunities, where traders buy low on one platform and sell high on another.
  • Fragmented Liquidity: The presence of $BBW on multiple platforms might fragment liquidity, which could affect price stability and trading volumes on your official platform.

Conclusion: A user bought $BBW and used SOL to create a concentrated liquidity pool on Raydium. While this makes $BBW more accessible for trading, you do not have information about whether this user is earning fees from the pool.
